# Gap Analysis Table

| Gap Type | Gap Identified | Evidence (Citation) |
|---|---|---|
| **Knowledge** | Peru has multiple studies on child anemia, but there is still no concise synthesis centered on the recent persistence of anemia among children aged 6 to 35 months using the 2019-2024 period as the main analytical window. | Sobrino et al. (2014); Velasquez-Hurtado et al. (2016) |
| **Methodological** | Most national studies are cross-sectional or limited to earlier ENDES windows; there is still room for a harmonized repeated cross-sectional analysis that explicitly addresses comparability concerns after the updated WHO 2024 / RM 251-2024-MINSA criteria. | Accinelli & Leon-Abarca (2020); Al-kassab-Cordova et al. (2023) |
| **Contextual** | The literature consistently shows high-burden clusters in rural, highland, and Amazonian territories, yet national discussion often remains too aggregated and insufficiently targeted. | Al-kassab-Cordova et al. (2022); Cerda-Hernandez et al. (2024) |
| **Policy / Implementation** | Peru has anti-anemia programs and interventions, but evidence suggests a gap between formal policy design and effective uptake, adherence, and sustained reduction in prevalence. | Berky et al. (2020); Whitney et al. (2021); Brewer et al. (2020) |
| **Analytical Contribution** | A stronger bridge is still needed between descriptive prevalence reporting and policy-relevant explanation of why anemia persists despite years of supplementation and multisectoral action. | Dorsey et al. (2021); ENDES 2024; MINSA 2024-2030 plan |
