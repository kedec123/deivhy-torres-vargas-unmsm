# Gap Analysis Table

| Gap Type | Gap Identified | Evidence (Citation) |
|---|---|---|
| **Knowledge gap** | There is relevant literature on child anemia in Peru, but there is still no short, clearly framed synthesis centered on recent persistence among children aged 6 to 35 months in the 2019-2024 window. | Sobrino et al. (2014); Velasquez-Hurtado et al. (2016) |
| **Methodological gap** | Many national analyses rely on earlier ENDES periods or do not foreground the comparability issue created by updated anemia criteria. A newer harmonized repeated cross-sectional analysis is still justified. | Accinelli and Leon-Abarca (2020); Al-kassab-Cordova et al. (2023) |
| **Territorial gap** | The literature shows clear spatial concentration, but public discussion often remains at the national average level and loses targeting precision. | Al-kassab-Cordova et al. (2022); Cerda-Hernandez et al. (2024) |
| **Implementation gap** | Existing policies and supplementation efforts do not automatically translate into consistent results, which suggests a continuing gap between formal intervention design and real uptake or effectiveness. | Berky et al. (2020); Brewer et al. (2020); Whitney et al. (2021) |
| **Contribution of the present study** | The proposed project can connect recent trend analysis with a more policy-relevant explanation of persistence, using a clearly bounded 2019-2024 frame and a stronger focus on inequality. | Dorsey et al. (2021); ENDES 2024; MINSA 2024-2030 plan |
